Madurai

A city of art, heritage, and devotion, Thanjavur is renowned for the majestic Brihadeeswarar Temple, the grand Thanjavur Maratha Palace, and its world-famous Tanjore paintings.

Thanjavur, often called the 'Rice Bowl of Tamil Nadu,' is a treasure trove of history and culture, reflecting the glory of the Chola dynasty.

Steeped in history and architectural brilliance, Thanjavur is home to the **Brihadeeswarar Temple**, a UNESCO World Heritage Site that stands as a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture. The city’s vibrant art scene, including the renowned Tanjore paintings, classical music, and dance, make it a cultural hub.

Thanjavur isn’t just about temples and history—it offers a deep cultural experience with its traditional handicrafts, serene countryside, and educational centers. From the grandeur of the **Thanjavur Maratha Palace** to the spiritual aura of its sacred shrines, the city welcomes travelers seeking a journey through time and tradition.

Explore Thanjavur's Wonders

Brihadeeswarar Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this Chola-era temple is an architectural marvel dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Thanjavur Maratha Palace

Once the royal residence of the Thanjavur Marathas, this palace complex houses museums, art galleries, and the Saraswathi Mahal Library.

Saraswathi Mahal Library

One of Asia’s oldest libraries, this repository contains rare palm-leaf manuscripts and ancient texts in multiple languages.

Gangaikonda Cholapuram

A historic temple built by Rajendra Chola I, reflecting the grandeur of Chola architecture and devotion.

Schwartz Church

Built in 1779 by the British, this historic church is a symbol of Thanjavur's colonial past and religious harmony.

Raja Raja Chola Statue

A tribute to the great Chola emperor, this statue stands as a reminder of Thanjavur’s glorious history.
